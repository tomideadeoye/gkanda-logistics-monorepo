IKORODU, Nigeria, October 22, 2025 — GK AND A LOGISTICS SERVICES LTD COMMENCES OPERATIONS AT NPA IKORODU EXPORT PROCESSING TERMINAL

## PRESS RELEASE

In keeping with the agenda of President Muhammadu Buhari's administration to diversify the national economy and reduce dependence on oil exports, GK and A Logistics Services Ltd today announced the commencement of operations at its export processing terminal located within the Nigerian Ports Authority (NPA) Lighter Terminal in Ibeshe-Ikorodu, Lagos State.

The facility, which officially opened its doors for business today, represents the first non-oil export processing terminal in the region and is strategically positioned to support the Federal Government's economic diversification objectives. GK and A Logistics Services Ltd is committed to providing world-class logistics solutions through the design, development, and operation of inland ports and related infrastructure that are efficient, sustainable, and economically impactful.

Speaking on behalf of GK and A Logistics Services Ltd, Omobola Abiru, Managing Director, emphasized the company's commitment to operational excellence and efficiency in the maritime logistics sector.

"Our operational terminal in Ikorodu represents more than just infrastructure – it's a strategic platform designed to reduce congestion and turnaround time in Lagos' port ecosystem," said Abiru. "We're providing immediate business operations for our partners while serving as a transitional platform for our upcoming regional inland port development."

The Ikorodu facility spans 20,000 square meters and serves as a crucial gateway for Nigeria's import and export activities. The terminal provides comprehensive logistics services including empty container processing, truck staging, cargo handling, and temporary storage.

Key features of the operational NPA Lighter Terminal facility include:
- Empty Container Processing Terminal (EPT) for specialized handling
- Holding Bay for truck staging and cargo movement coordination
- Comprehensive cargo handling and temporary storage services
- Seamless integration with ongoing port activities

The strategic location provides direct access to maritime shipping routes and connectivity to Lagos' extensive road network, positioning GK and A Logistics Services Ltd at the forefront of Nigeria's inland port evolution.

GK and A Logistics Services Ltd has secured all necessary environmental and regulatory approvals for both its current operations and future projects. The company is simultaneously developing the Ikorodu Regional Inland Port on 150 hectares of land, with expansion potential up to 667 hectares. This future-ready port hub will feature full terminal infrastructure, industrial cluster zones, and digital innovation services.

Bello Koko, Managing Director of NPA, had previously emphasized the importance of such facilities, stating that the rationale is to have a one-stop shop where all inspection, consolidation, and stuffing of export-bound cargo will be conducted in a synchronized manner to eliminate delays that have historically rendered Nigeria's non-oil exports uncompetitive.

The NPA management under his watch committed to effectively implementing all government policies geared towards the promotion of exports, as well as strengthening indigenous capacity through the promotion of local content.

GK and A Logistics Services Ltd is actively seeking partnerships with port operators, shipping lines, freight forwarders, and businesses engaged in import/export activities. The company welcomes both operational collaborations and strategic investment opportunities as it continues to build Nigeria's future in inland ports and logistics.
