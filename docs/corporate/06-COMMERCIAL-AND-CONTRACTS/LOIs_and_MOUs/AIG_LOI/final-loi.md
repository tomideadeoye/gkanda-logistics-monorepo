We are pleased to express our intent to engage Sensitive Professional Company Ltd to provide comprehensive security services at our terminal located at the Nigerian Ports Authority (NPA) Lighter Terminal, Ikorodu site, Lagos.
1. Background and Risk Context
The Ikorodu terminal has historically been utilized by the Nigerian Customs Service (NCS) for demurrage/overtime and seized cargo. This has created a perception of "abandoned" areas alongside the presence of high-value goods, making the location a theft hot spot.
As a waterfront facility, the site is particularly vulnerable to waterborne incursions. Intelligence reports indicate potential for coordinated approaches via the waterway, sometimes in large groups (reports of up to 40 individuals). As GK & A assumes control of part of the facility, we anticipate continued targeting. We therefore require visible, proactive measures—ashore and waterside—to deter theft, protect cargo, and maintain compliance with NPA regulations and the ISPS Code.
2. Leadership and Oversight
We specifically request that this engagement be overseen by AIG Abiodun Asabi (Rtd). We intend to draw upon your extensive law enforcement and security operations experience to guide the security posture, coordination, and performance expectations throughout this engagement.
3. Proposed Scope of Services
We require a full-spectrum security solution including, but not limited to:
Access Control: Positive ID checks, permit issuance, controlled gates/turnstiles, full entry/exit logs, and a strict visitor escort policy (no unauthorized access).
"Red Zone" Management: Restricted areas for seized/overtime cargo featuring a strict access list, two-person rule, 100% escorting, and a no-phone/no-bag policy.
Chain-of-Custody: Utilization of tamper-evident seals, dual sign-offs for handovers, sealed logbooks, and body-worn cameras during handling.
Perimeter Hardening:
Landside: Fence/gate repairs, removal of climb-aids, and razor coil installation where approved.
Waterside: Floating barriers/booms or nets, secured ladders, locked jetties, and clear "no-approach" signage.
Lighting: Rehabilitation of dark spots and installation of long-range LED floodlights on the waterfront.
Patrols & Quick Reaction:
Guard-tour system with GPS/NFC checkpoints; randomized day/night patrols.
Waterside patrol capability (own craft or coordinated patrols with Marine Police/LASWA), including defined patrol windows and interception procedures.
Quick Reaction Force (QRF) plan for mass-intrusion scenarios, with muster points, lockdown steps, and authority call-outs.
Surveillance: CCTV covering Red Zones, gates, perimeter, jetties, and waterfront approaches (including low-light/thermal capabilities) with 98% minimum uptime and 30+ day retention.
Incident Response: An escalation matrix integrating NPA Security, NCS, Marine Police, and (as applicable) Nigerian Navy/LASWA; marine VHF comms protocols.
Manpower: Vetted, licensed guards; supervisor on each shift; rotation protocols to reduce collusion risk; appropriate PPE and approved non-lethal equipment.
4. Equipment Requirements (As Applicable)
Radios with recorded comms; marine VHF sets for waterside teams.
Body-worn cameras; fixed/PTZ cameras (including thermal for waterfront).
Metal detectors; patrol vehicle(s); lighting towers and siren/PA systems.
Floating barriers/booms or other approved waterside deterrents.
