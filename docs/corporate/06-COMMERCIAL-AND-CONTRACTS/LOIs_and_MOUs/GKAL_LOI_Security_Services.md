Dear Sir/Madam,

We are pleased to express our intent to engage Sensitive Professional Company Ltd to provide security services at our terminal located at the Nigerian Ports Authority (NPA) Ikorodu site, Lagos.

**Background and Risk Context:**
The Ikorodu terminal has historically been underutilized and is used by the Nigerian Customs Service (NCS) for demurrage/overtime and seized cargo. This has created a perception of "abandoned" areas alongside the presence of high-value goods, making the location a theft hot spot. As a waterfront facility, the site is also vulnerable to waterborne incursions, including coordinated approaches via the waterway, sometimes in large groups (reports of up to 40 individuals). As we assume control of part of the facility, we anticipate continued targeting. We therefore require visible, proactive measures—ashore and waterside—to deter theft, protect cargo, and maintain compliance with NPA regulations and the ISPS Code.

**Leadership and Oversight:**
This engagement will be overseen by AIG Abiodun Asabi, Rtd. Drawing on his law enforcement and security operations experience, he will guide our security posture, coordination, and performance expectations throughout the engagement.

**Proposed Scope of Services:**

• **Access Control and Visitor/Contractor Management:**
  - Positive ID checks, permit issuance, controlled gates/turnstiles, full entry/exit logs and badge audits.
  - Strict visitor escort policy; no unauthorized access.

• **Restricted "Red Zones" for Seized and Overtime Cargo:**
  - Controlled access list, two-person rule, 100% escorting.
  - No-phone policy and no personal bags in Red Zones.

• **Chain-of-Custody Controls:**
  - Tamper-evident seals, dual sign-off for all handovers, sealed logbooks, body-worn cameras during handling.

• **Perimeter Hardening and Deterrence (Landside and Waterside):**
  - Fence and gate repairs/upgrades; removal of climb-aids; razor coil where approved.
  - Waterfront hardening: floating barriers/booms or nets where feasible, secured ladders, locked/controlled jetties and moorings, clear "no-approach" zones with signage.
  - Lighting rehabilitation to remove dark spots; long-range LED floodlights on the waterfront.

• **Patrols and Quick Reaction:**
  - Guard-tour system with GPS/NFC checkpoints; randomized day/night patrols.
  - Waterside patrol capability (own craft or coordinated patrols with Marine Police/LASWA), including defined patrol windows and interception procedures.
  - Quick Reaction Force (QRF) plan for mass-intrusion scenarios, with muster points, lockdown steps, and authority call-outs.

• **Surveillance and Monitoring:**
  - CCTV covering Red Zones, gates, perimeter, jetties, and waterfront approaches; low-light/thermal cameras on the waterside.
  - 30+ day retention; minimum 98% uptime; remote viewing for management.
  - Optional sensors: motion/beams on waterfront fence lines; horn/siren integration for deterrence.

• **Incident Response and Escalation:**
  - Clear escalation matrix integrating NPA Security, NCS, Marine Police, and (as applicable) Nigerian Navy/LASWA; evidence preservation procedures.
  - Marine VHF comms protocols and emergency call-out list.

• **Manpower and Supervision:**
  - Vetted, licensed guards; supervisor on each shift; rotation to reduce collusion risk; appropriate PPE and approved non-lethal equipment.

• **Reporting and Service Levels:**
  - Immediate reporting of critical incidents; daily occurrence book; weekly risk and KPI reports; post-incident debriefs and lessons learned.

• **Compliance and Training:**
  - Compliance with NPA by-laws, ISPS Code, and site HSE standards; site induction, NDAs, and periodic refresher training.
  - Drills for anti-intrusion/anti-boarding, night response, and mass-approach scenarios (up to 40 persons).

**Equipment (as applicable):**
• Radios with recorded comms; marine VHF sets for waterside teams.
• Body-worn cameras; fixed/PTZ cameras (including thermal for waterfront).
• Metal detectors; patrol vehicle(s); lighting towers and siren/PA systems.
• Floating barriers/booms or other approved waterside deterrents.
