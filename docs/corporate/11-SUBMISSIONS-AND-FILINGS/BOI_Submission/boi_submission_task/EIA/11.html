CHAPTER EIGHT: DECOMMISSIONING/CLOSURE PLAN

8.1 Introduction

Decommissioning and restoration are the two major activities at the tail end of a project lifecycle, while decommissioning refers to the process of winding up or closure of a running facility, restoration and/ or remediation is designed to ensure that the operation sites are left in an environmentally benign way. The proposed development has been designed for a minimum of0 years after which it could be demolished and reconstructed or converted to other uses.

8.2 Decommissioning

For this project, decommissioning shall be in two (2) phases. First phase will decommissioning after the construction phase of the proposed development. This will involves the disengagement and removal of all equipment used in the course of the project construction. Specifically, the phase of decommissioning shall include activities such as: * Dismantling of the temporary camp structures * Removal and transfer other equipment/ machineries * Removal and disposal of all wastes resulting from demolition of temporary structures

The Second Phase shall be carried out at the expiration of the proposed project lifespan. At the expiration of the proposed project lifespan of 50 years, the proponent shall decide to rehabilitate the facilities or decommission and convert the site to others use(s) or closed down the site.

These activities shall be conducted in strict compliance with all Health, Safety and Environment Standards and in line with the Environmental Management Plan developed for the project.

8.3 Site Restoration and Remediation

The remediation measures that need to be undertaken is expected to check the resultant negative impacts of decommissioning itself as this phase of the project, per se, is expected to have on the environment, hence a need for remediation action plans. The likely impacts include: * Physical disturbance arising from equipment removal and structure demolition techniques/ activities * Potential degradation of soil and groundwater quality * Hazards and mishaps related with decommissioning and movement of site equipment.

In the light of the features of the proposed project, the following measures shall be planned for implementation during decommissioning/closure: * Ensure proper and adequate drainage pattern and lines are done * Equipment and other tools used for operation in the facility must be removed from the project site. * Proper closure/ backfilling of all trenches * Restoration of ALL excavated areas to their near original status to minimize negative impacts. * An efficient Waste Management Plan shall be put in place and implemented * All mitigation Measures recommended for the decommissioning phase shall be carefully implemented * Suitable Disengagement Plan for worker *

CHAPTER NINE: CONCLUSION AND RECOMMEDATION

9.1 Conclusion

In the light of the detailed description of baseline environmental characteristics of the project area, and the exhaustive impact identification that have been presented in earlier sections of this report we can without fear make the following conclusions:

The proposed Container Terminal development have potential environmental and socio-economic benefits such as already enumerated in Chapters 2 and 5 of this report. The proposed project shall bolster the economic growth and prosperity of the Nigeria, businesses and workers.

Environmental Impacts such as noise, air pollution and contamination of soil, Surface and ground water may arise from the project during construction and operational phases. However, there are sufficient scientific/ engineering solutions available to effectively mitigate the impacts on the project that may arise in the course of implementation and they are already recommended as mitigation measures in Chapter 6 of the report. A detailed Environmental Management Plan has been prepared to ensure sustainability of the project. The Institutional framework that permits the implementation of the project make provision for monitoring by regulators. This allows for best practice in project implementation and avoidance of environmental mishap.

9.2 RECOMMEDATION

Based on the outcome of this Environmental Impact Assessment, the following recommendations considered essential for the sustainable implementation of the proposed project are hereby made:

  • Developmental activities should be limited to only land area required for the project and avoid further destruction of the remaining wetland.
  • The integration of the natural ecosystem into the proposed project design should be given adequate consideration as part of efforts to achieve ecosystem balance.
  • All Planning and Building permits required for the proposed development should be obtained from the relevant Agencies of Government.
  • The Mitigation measures and Environmental Management Plan captured in this EIA report should be adequately implemented.
  • All relevant Stakeholders particularly neighboring facilities should be engaged through a continued Stakeholders Engagement Process.

In the light of the foregoing, it is our considered opinion that if appropriate mitigation measures, particularly those recommended in Chapter six of this report are implemented and if the monitoring and management program for the environment and project are equally handled in the proper manner, the entire project would be environmentally sustainable. We therefore recommend that project be granted all necessary approvals and permit.
