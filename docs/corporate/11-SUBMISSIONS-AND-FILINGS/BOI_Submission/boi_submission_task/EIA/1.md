# **OUTLINE BUSINESS CASE: Establishment of a Regional Port in Ebute Ikorodu, Lagos, Nigeria**

**Introduction:**
The proposed project presents a collaborative initiative between GK&A Logistics Services Ltd. and the Nigerian Port Authority Lighter Port Terminal in Ebute Ikorodu, Lagos. The primary goal of the project is to spur economic growth, promote increased trade, create employment opportunities, and drive sustainable development in the region.

**Land:**
The NPA Lighter Terminal is an existing port in Ebute Ikorodu, Lagos, strategically granting access to maritime routes and providing an ideal setting for efficient port operations.

**Estimated Project Cost:**
The estimated project cost is approximately USD 2 million, covering various components such as land lease, design, construction, and equipment of the port facility, construction of warehouses, road pavement, fencing, CCTV installation, acquisition of computers, software, hardware, information technology licenses, purchase of operational vehicles, as well as salaries, allowances, and emoluments.

**Tenure:**
The proposed concession tenure for the regional port development spans 25 years, aligning with substantial investment, security considerations, and economic significance. Additionally, a request for the concession to be renewable for another 5 years has been made, ensuring long-term sustainability and continued contribution to the growth and stability of the economy.

**Project Description:**
The partnership entails the construction of a modernized existing regional port in Ikorodu, Lagos, emphasizing efficient operations and environmental sustainability. Financing has been secured, and collaboration with distinguished technical experts from Singapore and Shanghai has been established to ensure successful implementation and operation of the regional port.

**Justification:**
The establishment of a regional terminal in Ebute Ikorodu holds immense significance for the economic development of the region and Nigeria as a whole. It will generate employment opportunities, facilitate increased trade, improve logistics efficiency, and position Nigeria as a prominent player in the global trade network.

**Scope of Project:**
The project encompasses the design, construction, and operation of the regional port, including berths, cargo handling facilities, storage areas, and supporting infrastructure. Emphasis is placed on environmental sustainability to minimize the environmental impact of port operations, promoting eco-friendly measures and green port practices.

**Partnership Benefits:**
*   **Expertise and Experience:** GK&A Logistics Services Ltd. and its technical partners offer extensive expertise in logistics and port operations, drawing on the knowledge and experience of Singapore and Shanghai for the development of a world-class port in Nigeria.
*   **Economic Impact:** The regional port will attract investments, create job opportunities, stimulate economic activities, and position Nigeria as a hub for international trade.
*   **Diplomatic Ties and Knowledge Exchange:** The partnership provides an opportunity to strengthen ties and exchange knowledge with Singapore and Shanghai, enhancing Nigeria's maritime sector and positioning it as a key player in international trade.
*   **Increased Government Revenue:** The port partnership with the Federal Ministry of Marine is expected to generate more revenue for the government, providing additional financial resources for public projects and initiatives.

**Cargo Projection:**
The projected cargo throughput is expected to start at approximately 2 million tons per annum and is anticipated to increase to around 4 million tons within the first 7 years of operations, reflecting a substantial growth trajectory.

**Conclusion:**
The partnership between GK&A Logistics Services Ltd. and the Ministry of Marine and Blue Economy will establish a world-class regional port in Ebute Ikorodu, Lagos, Nigeria, driving economic growth, creating jobs, and contributing to sustainable development.

## **EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

### **1.0 INTRODUCTION**

**Background Information**

Nigeria has over the years become import-dependent for critical raw materials, finished goods, and other consumables with almost eighty percent of imports coming through the seaports, particularly Apapa and Tin-can Ports. With these Ports functioning with aged infrastructure and equipment, congestion became inevitable over the years resulting in freight operators spending an average of two weeks to get into the port. The port corridor now plays host to long queues of trucks trying to get into the ports to truck out containers. This congestion, which is almost crippling operations at the Lagos Ports, has compelled some shipping lines to divert Nigeria-bound cargoes to neighboring ports in Cotonou and Cote D'Ivoire, according to the Nigerian Ports Consultative Council. These diversions result in obvious economic losses to Nigeria running into billions of Naira in revenue that should have accrued to the Nation.

It is pertinent to mention that addressing this challenge that is impacting negatively not only the nation's revenue but also the logistic and supply chain sector of the economy requires a multi-faceted approach involving all stakeholders. Experts, practitioners, and Stakeholders in the Shipping and Maritime Sector have identified critical efforts required in addressing this challenge. These include Port concession to bring about efficiency, rehabilitation of Port access and inner roads, automation of port inspections, acquisition of new equipment to replace obsolete ones, and establishment of terminals outside the Port area.

It is in this regard that GK&A Logistics Services is proposing to support the Government's efforts at decongesting the Apapa and Tin Can Ports with the development of a Container Terminal at Ipakodo/Ebute-Iga waterfront of the Lagos Lagoon, in Ikorodu Local Government Area of Lagos State. To ensure the sustainable implementation of the proposed project and compliance with statutory regulatory requirements, the proposed Container Terminal is to be subjected to an Environmental Impact Assessment.

Consequently, GK&A Logistics Services commissioned Greenway Consult Limited to conduct the Environmental Impact Assessment of the proposed Container Terminal in fulfillment of regulatory requirements and in line with the Company HSE Policy.

The EIA included well-planned site visits, field survey and samples collection, laboratory analyses, and secondary data acquisition from existing reports and publications, as well as derivation of spatially referenced data using GIS and remote sensing technologies. Essentially, Environmental Impact Assessment is a decision-making tool which ensures that environmental and socio-economic impacts of a proposed project or activities are satisfactorily assessed and considered through planning to implementation of the proposed project or activity. It ensures that the "best" approach/alternative for the project is selected. In an EIA, broad elements of the environment of a proposed project are assessed, predictions are made of the possible change such a project could have on such elements of the environment, and measures to either eliminate or minimize (mitigate) the effects of such changes are proffered.

**Objectives of the Study**

The overall aim of this study was to establish a baseline inventory of the existing ecological status of the proposed project area, establish the potential associated biophysical and socio-economic impacts of the proposed activities, and develop an efficient, realistic, and implementable Environmental Management Plan (EMP). The essence is to incorporate the findings of the study in the project plan and activities with the aim of ensuring that the various elements of the environment are not adversely impacted while the beneficial impacts are well harnessed.

**Scope of the EIA**

To ensure a clear understanding and adequate coverage of the relevant issues in the EIA process, a Terms of Reference (TOR), which highlights key project components, general environmental conditions, objectives, and scope, was prepared for the proposed project. In the TOR, which was approved by the Lagos State Ministry of the Environment and Water Resources, the scope of the EIA was highlighted.

### **ES 2.0 PROJECT JUSTIFICATION AND ALTERNATIVES**

**Need for the Project**

The ever-growing maritime industry in Nigeria requires an innovative strategy to cope with the business volume. Statistics have shown a steady annual growth in cargo traffic within the Nigerian Ports, which stands between 5.5% - 6.6% (2007 - 2020). This is evident in the increase in the total number of containers recorded between 2014 - 2020 to about 20 million containers. The Ports in Nigeria, especially Lagos, have been stretched to their limits due to this increase in commercial activities. Technical capacity constraints, such as lack of storage space, unavailability of mooring posts, insufficient cargo handling equipment, and limits to traffic expansion, have continued to stagnate growth within the industry. The provision of efficient services will attract investors to harness the growth potentials in the local industry. This will invariably translate into economic buoyancy for the Nation at large. But first, the various challenges facing the industry, such as the traffic gridlock along the corridors of the Ports, long waiting periods for Vessels calling at the Nigerian Ports, etc., have to be squarely addressed. The resultant effect of these situations is the loss of productive man-hours and high cost of goods and services within the Country, resulting in hyper-inflation. The proposed Container Terminal, when completed, will address some of the enumerated challenges above in addition to socio-economic benefits like employment generation, reduction in social vices and malfeasance, additional revenue sources for the Government at various levels, etc.

### **ES 4.0 DESCRIPTION OF THE EXISTING PROJECT ENVIRONMENT**

**Study Approach**

The study approach adopted in this Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) study involved mainly the acquisition of ecological baseline data from a review of existing literature, field studies and laboratory analysis, interviews, and consultations with individuals, communities, and institutional stakeholders with respect to the proposed project and project area. This approach provided adequate information for establishing the baseline status of the proposed project environment. The scope of the study covered the following:

*   Meteorology
*   Air quality/Noise
*   Soil quality/land use
*   Groundwater resources
*   Vegetation
*   Wildlife
*   Geology and hydrogeology
*   Public and Institutional consultation
*   Socio-economic studies

One-season baseline data gathering was undertaken for the dry season (in line with the approved EIA's Terms of Reference by the Lagos State Ministry of the Environment and Water Resources) while the wet season (secondary data) was obtained from a combination of sources, including a review of literature (previous publications on the project environment and similar environmental studies around the project area). The field sampling was carried out between 11th and 13th February 2023, and analyses of the samples were done at Environmental Laboratories Limited, Ikosi, Lagos.

Important environmental, socio-economic, and health information (such as maps, charts, photographs) on the proposed project location were gathered during the fieldwork, while data gap analysis was carried out to identify areas where additional information will be required. The results were used in planning for the field survey and sampling strategy for this EIA study.

**Sampling Strategy and Field Data Collection**

The following was critical to the sampling strategy for the EIA study:

*   Likely impacted areas/locations during the construction and operational phase of the project
*   Vulnerability of environmental attributes during project operation
*   Buffer zone

Sampling activities were conducted based on a pre-determined sampling plan. The objectives of the sampling activities are to:
1.  Establish the existing state of the environment.
2.  Appraise the existence of sensitive environmental components such as shallow groundwater, sacred places and shrines, etc.
3.  Evaluate the sensitivities of the various environmental components to the planned project activities.

The sampling points for each of the environmental media were based on geomorphic and other environmental considerations of the study area. Global Positioning Systems (GPS) coupled with Remote Sensing (RS) and geo-spatial (GIS) technologies were used to spatially reference site and sampling points. The products of the GPS, RS, and GIS were integrated to produce the various thematic (sampling) maps for the various environmental parameters in order to relate the spatial distribution of the sampling activities with respect to the project environment.

The number of samples was determined by the type of data of interest and the relative variability of the sampling points. For example, while composite samples were taken for soil studies (top and sub-soil), the vegetation study (sampling) was based on a study of quadrants placed on representative areas of sample locations. However, the collection and laboratory analysis of all the field samples were conducted at Environmental Laboratories Limited, Ikosi-Ketu, Lagos.

**Field Studies and Laboratory Analysis**

Field studies were carried out between 11th and 13th November 2022 to collect relevant baseline data of the study area. The main objective of the field data acquisition was to establish the current biophysical, social, and health status of the local environment of the project through collection of air, soil, groundwater, surface water, plankton, benthos, and sediment samples, including ambient air quality and noise measurement. The baseline data acquisition exercise was undertaken for the dry season and was witnessed by officers from the Lagos State Ministry of the Environment and Water Resources.

Samples collected were analyzed at the Environmental Laboratories Limited at 28 Apaola Street, off Aladelola Street, Ikosi-Ketu, Lagos, and methodologies used for sample analyses were based on the American Society for Testing and Materials (ASTM) and American Public Health Association (APHA).

**Methodology and Determination of Parameters**

**Air Quality & Noise**

Air Quality is the measurement of the deviation of the ambient air from its natural state due to air pollution (the presence of undesirable material in the air, in quantities large enough to produce harmful effects). The assessment of the air quality of the study area was necessary in order to determine the air quality status of the area and investigate the possible contribution of the study environment activities to ambient air quality. The air quality parameters determined were mainly those of public and health concern.

**Noise Level**

The average noise level ranged between 41.0dB(A) and 68.0dB(A). The values recorded within the proposed project site (AQ1, AQ2 & AQ3) slightly exceeded the Noise Exposure Limits of the National Environmental Standard and Regulation Enforcement Agency (NESREA) for the land-use pattern for the proposed project location (60dBA). This is attributable to the noise emanating from vehicular movements within the proposed project area. The noise level recorded at sampling AQ4 and AQ5 are within the regulatory limits respectively.

**pH**

pH ranges from 6.40-6.58 with a mean average of 6.49 in the dry season study. A comparison with control samples indicated that the mean pH in the dry season sampling is lower than the samples in the control location.

**Electrical Conductivity (EC), Total Dissolved Solids (TDS), and Chloride**

The Electrical conductivity at the two sampling locations ranges from 7321µs/cm and 5348µs/cm with an average value of 6334.5µs/cm. The EC of the water for the control samples is 7220 µs/cm. The EC concentration values are influenced by the TDS and Chloride which had mean 948.5mg/l and 2736mg/l respectively. For all the sampling locations, the concentration of analysed parameters was relatively high, though mostly below the values recorded at the control which shows that the water is marine.

**Turbidity and Total Suspended Solids (TSS)**

Turbidity was 5.85NTU in sampling location 1 and 5.70NTU in sampling location 2, with an average value of 5.78. For the control area, turbidity was 5.90NTU. Meanwhile, Total Suspended Solids (TSS) ranged from 33.5-67.8 with a mean of 50.6 for the dry season sampling.

**Total Hydrocarbon**

The THC for both sampling points was 7.0mg/l and 9.0mg/l respectively, with an average value of 8.0mg/l. This is slightly lower than the value obtained at the control sampling point where a value of 9.0mg/l was recorded. The mean value obtained is lower than the regulatory limit of 10mg/l.

**Alkalinity and Bicarbonate**

Alkalinity had a mean concentration of 44.25 mg/l which is relatively lower than the value obtained in the control area. This is within the regulatory limit of 30-400mg/l for a water body.

**Heavy Metal Concentration**

The heavy metal concentrations of surface waters in the study area show that the concentration was slightly above the limits of the Nigerian Guidelines for aquatic life (FEPA, 1991) and levels known to harm biota. Iron ranged from 12.82 - 15.36ppm with a mean of 14.09ppm. Similar values were observed for other metals except lead, mercury, and vanadium which were lower than the stipulated limits in all locations.

**Chemical Oxygen Demand**

Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) ranged from 6.74-8.30mg/l with a mean of 7.52mg/l. This value is slightly lower than the one obtained in the control area.

**Dissolved Oxygen and Biochemical Oxygen Demand**

Dissolved Oxygen (DO) ranged from 4.51-4.22mg/l with a mean concentration of 4.37, which is slightly higher than the results recorded for the control location (4.25mg/l). Meanwhile, the Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) for the dry season ranged from 13.3-24.6mg/l and has a mean concentration of 18.95mg/l. The BOD of samples at the control area is 10.0mg/l. The higher BOD recorded at the proposed project sampled location is attributable to the fecal matters around the proposed project site which are occasionally washed into the water body, thereby bringing about eutrophication in the water body.

**Soil Quality Parameter**

The recorded concentration of cadmium in topsoil ranged from 0.43mg/kg to 2.47 mg/kg in all the sampling locations, with the highest values recorded in the SQ2 location. In sub-soil, the values ranged from 1.03 mg/kg to 2.05 mg/kg with the least recorded at (SQ3) and maximum at control points (SQ Control). Nickel and vanadium were not detected in any of the samples at all the study stations. Generally, the metal concentrations observed in the samples were lower than concentrations reported by Olukami and Adeoye, 2012 in soil and also lower than the reported natural background levels in soil by Robert and Grenato, 2000. Nevertheless, the presence of the metals could lead to the possibility of surface water pollution, especially through overland flow and/or by sub-surface water flow by percolation through the well-drained sandy soil of the study area during rainfall.

**Ground Water Quality Parameter**

The results of the analysis of groundwater samples showed that there were no Salmonella Sp, Shigella Sp, faecal Coliforms, nor heavy metals contamination of the groundwater for the two seasons. The samples were odourless, clear, and colourless but acidic in both dry and wet seasons. The conductivity of all the groundwater samples including the control (GW1-GW5) was within the WHO and NIS limits of 1000µS/cm. The presence of toxic metals in groundwater, including when present above their natural or background levels, could indicate contamination from anthropogenic influences or influences from the surrounding geologic formation of the soil. If present in high concentration in water, these metals pose health risks to living organisms including humans who consume the water. This could lead to debilitating diseases such as acute skin cancer and various organ malfunctions. Concentrations of Pb, Cu, Ni, Cd, and Cr were all below the detection limit of <0.001 mg/L. The observed concentrations of Fe in the groundwater at Station 1 at 0.31 mg/L was slightly higher than the recommended limit of 0.30 mg/L while those of station 2 at 0.06 mg/L was within range. Iron was not detected at Station 3 (GW3) at all. The observed Mn concentration was 0.48mg/L, 0.14mg/L, and 0.02 mg/L at Station 1 (GW1), Station 2 (GW2), and Station 3 (GW3) respectively. The Mn level at Station 1 (GW1) was higher than the SON-recommended limit. Other metals were all below the threshold prescribed by SON. Generally, the results indicate that the groundwater sources have not been contaminated by heavy metals.

**Microbiology Parameter of Groundwater**

The total coliform counts were all below the SON threshold of 10. While the Total Plate Count was 3.0 x 10¹ cfu at Station 1 and 4.0 x 10¹ cfu at Station 3 respectively, the count at Station 2 was < 2. Total coliform, faecal coliform, and E. Coli were all less than 2, indicating that the groundwater resources have not been contaminated by microbes, most especially human fecal coliform.

### **ES 5.0 IMPACT ANALYSIS**

**Potential Positive Impacts of the Proposed Project**

There are some positive impacts likely to arise from the proposed project. The basic and most critical positive impacts include the following:

*   Creation of Employment Opportunities
*   Stimulation of Economic Growth
*   Reduction in Crime and Social Vices
*   Increase in Revenue to Government

**Potential Negative Impacts**

**Site Preparation/Construction Phase Impacts**

*   **Impacts on Air Quality:** Air-borne particulates would result from disturbances to the soil during construction activities of the proposed project. For instance, a lot of dust would be generated during pilling. All diesel-powered facilities such as electricity generators and heavy-duty equipment as well as vehicles would emit SO₂, CO₂, NOX, and other gases. Degradation of air quality could occur due to emissions from vehicles. The emissions are short-term and localized within the immediate project environment.
*   **Impact on Noise Level:** During construction, a lot of noise will be generated particularly during foundations, pilling, iron cutting, and welding works as well as vehicular movement. Noise levels arising from these activities, though expected to exceed ambient levels at times, will be transient and localized. Given the fact that noise levels are not expected to significantly exceed the existing background and the fact that the area is already quite used to high levels of noise from vehicular movements, the sensitivity of the receptor is classified as minor and the overall risk level is expected to be low, since the impact is expected to be temporary.
*   **Impacts on Geology (Alteration to Site Geology):** The study area lies in a very geological (both tectonic and seismic) stable region with no recorded cases of earthquakes or tremors. In addition, no major or minor geological hazards are known to occur in the area. Only minimal geological impacts are expected in the construction phase of the project, which is related basically to the exaction of topsoil for construction works. No topographic or geologic alteration due to the construction of the proposed development is expected.
*   **Impacts on Soil:** The potential impacts on soil resources are closely related to site clearing, site preparation, and construction. These could manifest mostly as soil erosion, land degradation, and fugitive dust released into the atmosphere. Other significant potential effects on the soil include compaction, changes in drainage patterns, and contamination from discharges, leakages, and accidental releases. It is also expected that grading and compaction of soil would lead to loss of organic matter, alteration of soil structure, and reduction in filtration, water holding capacity, aeration, and root penetration. Alteration to soil conditions can cause widespread secondary impacts by reducing the capacity of the habitat to support flora and fauna.
*   **Impacts on Groundwater:** There is potential for percolation of materials such as oils, used lubricants, and wastes if not properly stored. This possibility is remote considering the site management plan that will be put in place. Therefore, the impact is considered negligible negative with low likelihood. The overall risk rating is low.
*   **Impacts on Waste Management:** A lot of wastes would be generated during the construction phase of the project. These would include metal cuttings, packaging material, anti-corrosion chemicals and paints, empty drums and tins, welding electrodes, etc. Human wastes generated from the construction workers could constitute aesthetic issues for the project area. Wastes, if allowed to accumulate, could lead to contamination of soil and groundwater, particularly if percolation and run-off into the waters are allowed. All wastes arising from construction activities will be evacuated from the site, for proper disposal by the Lagos State Waste Management Authority (LAWMA) or its appointed contractor.
*   **Impacts on Land-Use and Availability:** The proposed project site is zoned residential and there already exist within the area residential developments. The siting of the proposed project in the area would only enhance the compatibility of the land use with the existing land uses in the area.
*   **Impacts on Traffic and Transportation:** In the course of constructing the proposed Structure, there will be a need to transport materials and men to the project site at various times. In addition, other project components such as foundation equipment will also be transported to the site. The increased presence of heavy-duty trucks associated with the construction activities may further worsen the already difficult traffic situation in the area and could increase the potential for accidents. This could lead to physiological stress on the people in the area.
*   **Impacts on Public Health and Safety:** The construction phase of the project would definitely bring about the movement of different people including skilled and unskilled labour force and service providers. This has its own attendant health and safety problems.

**Operation/ Maintenance Phase Impacts**

*   **Impacts on Air Quality and Climate:** During operation, direct and negative impacts could result from generating sets, and vehicles traveling to and from the project site. In this regard, the impact of operations of the terminal facility on air quality is rated as a minor risk, with a minor negative consequence and low likelihood of occurrence.
*   **Impacts on Noise Level:** The noise that shall be generated from the operation of the proposed Container Terminal development is not expected to be above the recommended 70 decibels by NESREA for a commercial area as there would hardly be any activities that could generate such a level of noise. It would be safe therefore to conclude that impacts due to noise levels shall be low with a low likelihood of occurrence and low-risk impact.
*   **Impacts on Soil:** Potential impacts to soil during the operational phase could occur through the improper use, storage, and handling of materials such as petroleum products and solid wastes generated on site. The comprehensive environmental procedures prepared for the project will ensure that any potential impacts are adequately mitigated. Thus, the impact level is a minor negative consequence, with a low likelihood of occurrence and the overall risk level is adjudged low.
*   **Impacts on Groundwater:** During the operational phase, groundwater resources could be negatively impacted through leakage of petroleum product storage facilities on site or accidental spills. The management plan that shall be put in place will make spills and accidental discharges a very rare case. Also, the seepage of sewage/wastewater into the ground could impact negatively on the groundwater in the project area.
*   **Impacts on Transportation:** The transportation impacts resulting from the operation of the proposed development will be in terms of an increase in vehicular traffic as a result of residents and visitors to the facility. The magnitude of this impact will be moderate. The truth is that the project area is already subject to substantial vehicular traffic, the add-on effect on traffic will be minimal, even though the effect on the population cannot be ignored. In this regard, the severity of the impact in terms of the sensitivity of the receptor is rated moderate.
*   **Impact on Security Arrangement of the Project Area:** The existence of the proposed development in the project area could bring about new security challenges as more people and businesses will be attracted to the project area. This may result in an increase in crimes and social vices in the area and will obviously impact the existing security arrangement in the project area and stretch the host community's security infrastructure.
*   **Impact on Existing Infrastructure/Amenities:** The existence of the proposed development in the project area could put additional pressure on the existing infrastructure/utilities in the area such as roads, hospitals, schools, shopping malls, recreational facilities, and waste management facilities amongst others. For instance, the Connection of the facility to the national grid for electricity supply will increase electricity consumption in the project area.

### **ES 6.0 MITIGATION MEASURES**

**Mitigation Measures**

**Impact on Air quality**
GK &A Logistics Limited shall implement the following:

*   Measures to reduce dust, such as water suppression, shall be used where possible.
*   Regular maintenance and servicing of construction equipment that contribute to air emissions shall be implemented.
*   Only modern and well-maintained equipment and machinery for construction activities.
*   Movements of men and materials shall be properly coordinated to optimize vehicle use and reduction of resultant vehicular emissions.
*   All mobile and stationary internal combustion engines shall be properly maintained and put in good condition before and during project implementation.
*   Low emission/high-efficiency engines shall be used to reduce the emission of noxious gases.
*   All project vehicles shall be put off while in traffic jams to avoid the continuous release of emissions into the environment.
*   Appropriate PPEs including Nose masks shall be provided and their use by workers on site shall be enforced.

**Impact on Noise Level**
GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

*   All equipment is maintained as per manufacturer's recommendations and operated as per original specifications.
*   Activities associated with high levels of noise are limited to daylight hours.
*   Construction equipment with lower sound power levels is selected and used.
*   Project personnel use appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) to reduce exposure to noise impact (e.g., mufflers).
*   Noise attenuation measures such as the installation of acoustic mufflers on large engines and equipment are put in place.

**Impact on Terrestrial Ecosystem (Loss of Biodiversity and destruction of natural Habitats)**

*   Clearing of vegetation is limited to the area required for construction works.
*   Site clearing is carried out in a manner that will allow some of the terrestrial fauna within the project site to migrate or escape to other areas.
*   Work areas are clearly marked to prevent clearing or disturbance outside areas designated for work or for clearing.
*   Areas that are cleared but not used for the project shall be restored and revegetated. Revegetation procedures shall use native species where possible and control of invasive plants, including removal and replacement of existing invasive plants.

**Impact on Soil and Groundwater**
GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

*   Clearing and removal of vegetation is limited to only those areas required for Project activities.
*   Exposed areas of soil are revegetated as soon as possible.
*   Areas of exposed soil and soil stockpiles are reduced as much as practicable.
*   Excavation works are not to be executed under aggressive weather conditions.
*   Disturbed areas are rehabilitated as soon as possible to prevent erosion.
*   Work areas are clearly defined and, where necessary, demarcated to avoid unnecessary disturbance of areas outside the project footprint.
*   Any hydrocarbons, fuels, lubricants, and chemicals to be used are stored in properly labelled oil storage tanks (surrounded by a bund wall), with hoses and gauges kept within the bund.
*   Waste storage areas are located on hard standing (or in a bund wall, where necessary) to prevent potential contamination of soil and groundwater.
*   Spill containment equipment to contain incidental spills/leaks, including drip pans to contain any spills during refueling activities, is provided.
*   Spill control and response plans to respond to spills and leaks are implemented.
*   General wastes that cannot be reused are periodically evacuated by an approved waste disposal service provider.

**Impact on Surface Water and Hydrobiology**
GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

### **ES 7.0 ENVIRONMENTAL MANAGEMENT PLAN**

**Monitoring Schedule and Parameters**

Monitoring duration of all the parameters in the sand dredging project shall be for the life of the project. Much of the monitoring and data generation shall be on an on-site basis. The parameters to be measured and the frequency of measurement are indicated below:

**Air Quality and Noise**
Air quality parameters that will be measured shall include:

*   Particulates
*   Noxious gases (NO₂, CO, SO₂, VOC)
*   Noise levels

Locations for monitoring shall be within the project site, upwind, and downwind of the premises. As much as possible, in reporting the findings from the monitoring, measurements shall be compared with regulatory limits as well as the baseline data presented in Chapter five of this project.

**Water Quality**

Groundwater samples will be collected from boreholes and analysed for significant parameters. The parameters that will be measured include the following:

*   pH, Conductivity, TDS, Dissolved Oxygen, and Biochemical Oxygen Demand
*   Total Suspended Solids, Turbidity
*   Total hydrocarbons

Monitoring shall be conducted in at least 4 points: one point within the immediate vicinity of the project site, and one point upstream (control point).

**Socio-economics**

Socio-economic parameters are not as easily measurable as physico-chemical and biological indices. Given the various interfaces between the project and the socio-economy, as described in chapter four of this report, the main socio-economic index that can be monitored is traffic. As such, the baseline traffic situation will be established, prior to the commencement of operational activities. Traffic will be monitored on a weekly basis during site preparation and on a quarterly basis when the project is operational.

**Health and Safety of Workers**

Operations at all work sites shall be subject to Government, industry, and company policies and guidelines. All Company and contractor staff shall be well informed and trained on the Health and Environmental Safety policies and guidelines. All facilities shall also be designed to enhance safety planning, and activities shall be executed within the confines of relevant legislation and stakeholders' interests. All construction work by contractors shall be properly managed through careful planning and the application of relevant Health, Safety, and Environmental policies including the following:

*   Use of Permit to Work (PTW)
*   Job Hazard Analysis and toolbox meetings
*   Use of PPE in designated hazard areas
*   Prohibition of alcohol during work hours and at work sites and facilities
*   Regular emergency drills

**Waste Disposal Guidelines**

All debris, spoil materials, rubbish, and other waste, except excavated soil and rock, shall be cleared regularly from the sites and disposed of at Government-designated areas and facilities. Instructions on material safety handling sheets shall be strictly adhered to and shall be properly packaged and returned to manufacturers for proper disposal. Wastes in transit must be accompanied and tracked by consignment notes. The waste consignment notes shall contain the following as a minimum:

*   Date of dispatch
*   Description of waste
*   Waste quantity/container type
*   Consignee/driver name and means of transportation
*   Confirmation of actual disposal (time and date)

**Stakeholders Engagement**

Sustained Stakeholder Consultation and engagement shall be undertaken during all phases of the project. A robust Stakeholder Forum shall be established including directly affected parties, representatives from the local municipality, and other identified persons. The forum shall serve to communicate project progress, material changes to the project, grievances received, and corrective action taken.

### **ES 8.0 DECOMMISSIONING/CLOSURE PLAN**

**Decommissioning**

For this project, decommissioning shall be in two (2) phases. The first phase will be decommissioning after the construction phase of the proposed development. This will involve the disengagement and removal of all equipment used in the course of the project construction. Specifically, this phase of decommissioning shall include activities such as:

*   Dismantling of the temporary camp structures
*   Removal and transfer of other equipment/machineries
*   Removal and disposal of all wastes resulting from the demolition of temporary structures

The Second Phase shall be carried out at the expiration of the proposed project lifespan. At the expiration of the proposed project lifespan of 50 years, the proponent shall decide to rehabilitate the facilities or decommission and convert the site to other use(s) or close down the site.

These activities shall be conducted in strict compliance with all Health, Safety, and Environment Standards and in line with the Environmental Management Plan developed for the project.

**Site Restoration and Remediation**

The remediation measures that need to be undertaken are expected to check the resultant negative impacts of decommissioning itself as this phase of the project, per se, is expected to have on the environment, hence a need for remediation action plans. The likely impacts include:

*   Physical disturbance arising from equipment removal and structure demolition techniques/activities
*   Potential degradation of soil and groundwater quality
*   Hazards and mishaps related to decommissioning and movement of site equipment.

In light of the features of the proposed project, the following measures shall be planned for implementation during decommissioning/closure:

*   Ensure proper and adequate drainage patterns and lines are done.
*   Equipment and other tools used for operation in the facility must be removed from the project site.
*   Proper closure/backfilling of all trenches.
*   Restoration of ALL excavated areas to their near-original status to minimize negative impacts.
*   An efficient Waste Management Plan shall be put in place and implemented.
*   All mitigation Measures recommended for the decommissioning phase shall be carefully implemented.
*   Suitable Disengagement Plan for workers.

### **ES 9.0 CONCLUSION AND RECOMMENDATION**

**Conclusion**

In light of the detailed description of baseline environmental characteristics of the project area, and the exhaustive impact identification that have been presented in earlier sections of this report, we can without fear make the following conclusions:

The proposed Container Terminal development has potential environmental and socio-economic benefits such as already enumerated in Chapters 2 and 5 of this report. The proposed project shall bolster the economic growth and prosperity of Nigeria, businesses, and workers.

Environmental Impacts such as noise, air pollution, and contamination of soil, Surface and groundwater may arise from the project during construction and operational phases. However, there are sufficient scientific/engineering solutions available to effectively mitigate the impacts on the project that may arise in the course of implementation and they are already recommended as mitigation measures in Chapter 6 of the report. A detailed Environmental Management Plan has been prepared to ensure the sustainability of the project. The Institutional framework that permits the implementation of the project makes provision for monitoring by regulators. This allows for best practice in project implementation and avoidance of environmental mishaps.

**Recommendation**

Based on the outcome of this Environmental Impact Assessment, the following recommendations considered essential for the sustainable implementation of the proposed project are hereby made:

*   Developmental activities should be limited to only the land area required for the project and avoid further destruction of the remaining wetland.
*   The integration of the natural ecosystem into the proposed project design should be given adequate consideration as part of efforts to achieve ecosystem balance.
*   All Planning and Building permits required for the proposed development should be obtained from the relevant Agencies of Government.
*   The Mitigation measures and Environmental Management Plan captured in this EIA report should be adequately implemented.
*   All relevant Stakeholders, particularly neighboring facilities, should be engaged through a continued Stakeholders Engagement Process.

In light of the foregoing, it is our considered opinion that if appropriate mitigation measureecommended in Chapter six of this report, are implemented and if the monitoring and management program for the environment and project are equally handled in the proper manner, the entire project would be environmentally sustainable. We, therefore, recommend that the project be granted all necessary approvals and permits.
