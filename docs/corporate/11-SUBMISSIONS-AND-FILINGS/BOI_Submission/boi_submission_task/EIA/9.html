CHAPTER SIX: MITIGATION MEASURES

6.1 Introduction

Typically, the acceptability and/or suitability of a particular project is premised on several considerations, not the least of which is the reduction of negative environmental and social impacts to tolerable levels. Impact significance reduction is. usually achieved by introducing mitigation/amelioration measures to cater for the negative impacts identified. In this section of the report, we present a summary of those measures that are deemed adequate to achieve this objective as well as the residual impacts that will remain after the implementation of these mitigation measures. For ease of comprehension; the mitigation measures are presented based on the various impacts identified. It should be noted that no mitigation measures are necessary for positive impacts and as such they are not discussed in this section.

6.2 Mitigation Measures

Mitigation measures are options that can be used to either completely eliminate or minimize identified negative impacts of a development project. The traditional approach to design and operations is to ensure compliance with the applicable safety codes and standards during design. However, compliance with regulations, codes and standards may not be sufficient to achieve an appropriate level of Health Safety and Environmental (HSE) performance in design. Design codes are generic and applicable to facilities in a number of geographical areas that face a wide range of technical challenges unique to the project. The design of the proposed project is based on the strictest of international codes and best-practices. The HSE objective with respect to the design and building plan is to implement all cost effective measures to reduce the risk and effects from major hazards including accidents. The approach has been to use this as a goal rather than a prescriptive objective that cannot be achieved without following a documented process of identification, assessment, reduction and continuous monitoring. Thus the steps taken in the HSE process for the Project included the following:

  • Design based on Codes, Standards and Regulations.
  • Improved design based on Quantitative Risk Assessment and Environmental Impact Assessment
  • Improved design from human factors evaluation

It should be cleared here that most of the likely impacts due to this project have been considered in the selection of the conceptual design. While additional measures may require outright purchase of abatement equipment, others shall be requiring sound operational procedures and good maintenance. The selection of the proposed site has the potential to assist in avoiding or eliminating many possible impacts that might occur from a project of this nature, especially with reference to its proximity to like uses and being within an high profile commercial zone with less residential activities.

Site Preparation and Construction Phase

Impact on Air quality

GK &A Logistics Limited shall implement the following;

  • Measures to reduce dust, such as water suppression, shall be used where possible.
  • Regular maintenance and servicing of construction equipment that contribute to air emission shall be implemented.
  • Only modern and well maintained equipment and machinery for construction activities
  • Movements of men and materials shall be properly coordinated to optimize vehicle use and reduction of resultant vehicular emissions.
  • All mobile and stationary internal combustion engines shall be properly maintained and put in good condition before and during project implementation
  • Low emission/high efficiency engines shall be used to reduce emission of noxious gases
  • All project vehicles shall be put off while in traffic jams to avoid continuous release of emission into the environment
  • Appropriate PPEs such including Nose mast shall be provided and the use by workers on site shall be enforced

Impact on Noise Level

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

  • All equipment are maintained as per manufacturer’s recommendations and operated as per original specifications
  • Activities associated with high levels of noise are limited to daylight hours.
  • Construction equipment with lower sound power levels are selected and used.
  • Project personnel are used appropriate Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) to reduce exposure to noise impact (e.g. mufflers)
  • Noise attenuation measures such as installation of acoustic mufflers on large engines and equipment are put in place

Impact on Terrestrial Ecosystem (Loss of Biodiversity and destruction of natural Habitats)

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure

  • Clearing of vegetation is limited to the area required for construction works.
  • Site clearing is carried out in a manner that will allow some of the terrestrial fauna within the project site migrate or escape to other areas
  • Work areas are clearly marked to prevent clearing or disturbance outside areas designated for work or for clearing.
  • Areas that are cleared but not used for the project shall be restored and revegetated. Revegetation procedures shall use native species where possible and control of invasive plants, including removal and replacement of existing invasive plants.

Impact on Soil and Ground water

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

  • Clearing and removal of vegetation is limited to only those areas required for Project activities.
  • Exposed areas of soil are revegetated as soon as possible.
  • Areas of exposed soil and soil stockpiles are reduced as much as practicable.
  • Excavation works are not be executed under aggressive weather conditions.
  • Disturbed areas are rehabilitated as soon as possible to prevent erosion.
  • Work areas are clearly defined and where necessary demarcated to avoid unnecessary disturbance of areas outside the project footprint
  • Any hydrocarbons, fuels, lubricants and chemicals to be used are stored in properly labelled oil storage tanks (surrounded with bund wall), with hoses and gauges kept within the bund.
  • Waste storage areas are located on hard standing (or in a bund wall, where necessary) to prevent potential contamination of soil and ground water
  • Spill containment equipment to contain incidental spills/leaks, including drip pans to contain any spills during refueling activities are provided
  • Spill control and response plans to respond to spills and leaks are implemented.
  • General wastes that cannot be reused are periodically evacuated by approved waste disposal service provider

Impact on Surface Water and Hydrobiology

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

  • Proper drainage facilities are provided to prevent flooding. The run off shall be channeled to an oil/water separator and treated to NESREA and LASEPA standards before discharge
  • Excavated portions of the soils in the area are properly compacted to avoid mass movements of loose soil materials into the water course in the areas by all the possible agents of denudation
  • Cleared vegetation/materials are properly taken care of not to be washed into surface water course arbitrarily
  • Proper sewage and waste management facilities are put in place for workers, such as mobile toilets, waste bins so as not to leave the workers to self-help.
  • Any hydrocarbons, fuels, lubricants and chemicals to be used are stored in properly labelled oil storage tanks (surrounded with bund wall), with hoses and gauges kept within the bund.
  • Spill containment equipment to contain incidental spills/leaks, including drip pans to contain any spills during refueling activities are provided
  • Spill control and response plans to respond to spills and leaks are implemented.

Impact on Waste Management

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure

  • An efficient Waste Management Plan are put in place and implemented
  • Waste generated from the site preparation activities are properly stored to avoid contamination of ground and surface water.
  • Waste generated from site preparation activities are disposed off using approved Waste Disposal Service Provider

Workmen Safety (Injuries and Fatalities)

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

  • Appropriate Personnel Protective Equipment shall be provided and the used shall be enforced
  • First Aid Box and First Aiders shall be provided on site
  • Medical Facilities such as retainer hospital shall be provided to handle critical medical cases among workmen on site

Impact on Public Health, Safety and Security

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

  • The local communities are informed of the project activities prior to commencement of work.
  • Construction contractor are briefed on the socio-cultural norms and sensitivities of the neighboring communities before commencement of work in the area.
  • Construction workers camp are developed and workforce management protocols that include workforce behaviour and interactions with the local communities shall be implemented and enforced.
  • Community based training on the prevention of common communicable diseases, water protection/purification techniques and basic sanitation are organized periodically programme, including a Code of Conduct, for all construction workers (including new comers directly related to the project) shall be carried out prior to construction activities. This will increase sensitivity to local norms and customs and will provide awareness to contractors and employees of appropriate and acceptable behaviours and govern worker interactions with the local communities.
  • A grievance resolution mechanism (which tracks grievances and responses; and respond in a timely manner with corrective actions identified where appropriate) are developed and implemented.
  • Prohibition of unauthorized access to the site are maintained.
  • No employment shall take place at the entrance to the site. Only formal channels for employment are used, and recruitment shall take place only at designated times and locations.
  • An HIV/AIDS policy and information document for all workers directly related to the project are developed and implemented. The information document will address factual health issues as well as behaviour change issues around the transmission and infection of HIV/AIDS. Awareness education about HIV and AIDS and other sexually transmitted diseases is created among the workforce
  • Journey Management Plans that take into consideration peak hour travelling times during the day and try to avoid travelling during this time is developed and implemented

Impact on Traffic and Transportation

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure:

  • Journey Management Plans that take into consideration peak hour travelling times during the day and try to avoid travelling during this time is implemented
  • Drivers shall adhere to speed limits of less than 40 km/h in the project area.
  • Drivers’ competency is assessed and where required training shall be provided.
  • Visible warning signs are put on roads and vehicles associated with the project

GK &A Logistics Limited shall ensure

    • Fire hydrants with monitors located strategically throughout the Terminal
