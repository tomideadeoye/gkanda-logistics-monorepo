# Emergency Response Plan

## Executive Statement

<div style="page-break-after: always;"></div>

Safety, environmental protection, and legal compliance take precedence over production. This Plan is compulsory. Any deviation without prior written approval from the NPA Safety Director and the GK & A Managing Director is a breach subject to disciplinary action, removal from site, and contract termination for cause.

---

## Introduction

This Plan is a jointly endorsed, auditable operational control developed under Nigerian Ports Authority (NPA) oversight. Procedures, notification chains, and role interfaces have been reviewed with NPA and are mandatory for all personnel and contractors on GK & A-controlled areas of the NPA Lighter Terminal, Ikorodu.

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Enforcement** | Roles and accountabilities are assigned in writing. Compliance is continuously monitored. Violations are escalated through disciplinary protocols and may trigger regulatory reporting to NPA and relevant authorities. |
| **Joint Execution** | GK & A and NPA Safety Officers shall co-lead emergency activations, joint drills, audits, debriefs, and corrective actions. NPA retains authority to intervene and direct operations where necessary to protect life, environment, and port security. |

---

## 1. Purpose

This Plan establishes an integrated, zero-harm emergency management framework to:

| Objective | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Protection | Protect people, property, cargo integrity, and the environment |
| Prevention | Prevent escalation and minimize business interruption |
| Compliance | Achieve and evidence compliance with NPA directives, ISPS Code, IMDG Code, ISO 45001 and ISO 14001 principles, ILO/IMO Code of Practice for Safety and Health in Ports, Lagos State and Federal environmental obligations |
| Standards | Apply the most stringent requirement when standards overlap |

### Measurable Objectives

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Fatalities | Zero |
| Serious Injuries | Zero (life-altering) |
| Notification Time | Initiated within 5 minutes of emergency declaration |
| Headcount Reconciliation | Full headcount within 10 minutes of evacuation order |
| Drill Participation | ≥ 90% of on-duty personnel |
| Corrective Action Closure | ≤ 30 days |
| Spill Containment | No uncontrolled discharge to water; all spills contained within site boundary |

## 8. Emergency Response Procedures

### General Priorities

| Priority | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 1. Protect Life | Immediate protection of personnel |
| 2. Isolate Hazards | Contain and control the incident |
| 3. Prevent Escalation | Stop the incident from worsening |
| 4. Protect Environment | Prevent environmental damage |
| 5. Preserve Evidence | Maintain evidence for investigation |

### 8.1 Fire

| Aspect | Procedure |
|--------|-----------|
| PPE | Full structural gear and SCBA for interior/close approach |
| Electrical Fires | Isolate power first if safe; use CO2/DCP; avoid water on live components |
| Flammable Liquid Fires | Apply foam; protect drains; deploy booms to capture runoff; avoid fluorinated foams unless no alternative; prefer fluorine-free foam (F3) |
| Warehouse/Stack Fires | Establish 360° size-up; protect exposures; set collapse zones; use TIC to confirm knockdown; ventilate cautiously to prevent flashover; compartment doors stay closed until cooled and monitored |
| Post-fire | Fire watch 60 minutes minimum; water/runoff containment and sampling; document all actions and foam use |

### 8.2 Hazardous Spills/HAZMAT

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Approach | Approach upwind/upstream; identify via SDS/IMDG; don chemical-resistant PPE; consider SCBA for unknown/toxic atmospheres |
| Control | Stop the source; isolate ignition sources; ground/bond for flammables; deploy drain covers, booms, absorbents, temporary bunds; protect watercourses |
| Collection | Overpack drums, labeled containers; segregate waste; store in designated HAZMAT area for licensed disposal |
| Notifications | NPA Port Control; LASEPA/NESREA where thresholds met; record volumes and materials |
| Decontamination | Establish gross decon for responders/casualties when required before EMS handover |

### 8.3 Refrigerant Leaks (Reefer Stacks)

#### Hazards

| Hazard | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| A1 | Oxygen displacement |
| A2L | Mild flammability; ignition risk; decomposition products if heated |

#### Immediate Controls

| Control | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Evacuation | Evacuate immediate area; mark no-ignition zone |
| Cordon | Minimum cordon: 50 m if concentration and wind unknown; adjust based on readings |
| Hot Work | Prohibit hot work; isolate electrical power to affected unit(s) |

#### Monitoring

| Equipment | Procedure |
|-----------|-----------|
| Leak Detector | Use calibrated refrigerant leak detector (NDIR/IR for halocarbons) plus multi-gas meter |
| Alarm Set-points | Warn at 10% LFL for A2L; evacuate/cordon at ≥20–25% LFL; manage A1 as asphyxiant—use SDS OEL (often ~1,000 ppm TWA; confirm per SDS) |

#### Response

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Ventilation | Ventilate naturally or mechanically; ensure continuous ventilation in reefer alleys; interlock hot work shutdown where fixed detection exists |
| Repair | Only qualified technicians to isolate/repair; recover refrigerant to approved cylinders; record loss and service details |

#### Re-occupy Only When

| Parameter | Acceptable Range |
|-----------|------------------|
| O2 | 19.5–23.5% |
| LEL | < 10% |
| Refrigerant | Below OEL per SDS |
| Stability | Two consecutive stable readings 5–10 minutes apart |

### 8.4 Medical Emergencies

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Scene Safety | Ensure scene safety; announce "Medical Emergency – General Hospital Ikorodu"; deploy AED/trauma kit |
| First Aid | DRSABC; control bleeding; burns: cool with clean water (≥20 minutes); chemical exposures: flush ≥15 minutes and consult SDS |
| Heat Stress | Remove from heat, active cooling, hydration, monitor vitals |
| EMS Coordination | Coordinate LASAMBUS/112/767; guide ambulance through designated gate; ensure casualty/witness logs; decon equipment after use |

### 8.5 Security Incidents

| Incident Type | Response |
|---------------|----------|
| Lockdown | Lockdown/access control per PFSP; badge deactivation if required; ensure CCTV preservation and evidence handling |
| Bomb/Suspicious Package | Follow checklist; evacuate upwind; avoid radios within 15 m of suspect device |
| Intrusion/Theft | Do not pursue; preserve scene; notify NPA Port Police |
| Civil Disturbance | Shelter-in-place; safe room protocols; code words for silent alerts; comply with law enforcement |
| Communications | PIO manages all external comms; staff prohibited from social media posting |

### 8.6 Severe Weather and Flooding

#### Monitoring

| Parameter | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Alerts | Lightning alerts; wind thresholds; tidal/surge data |

#### Actions

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Secure Assets | Secure loose cargo/equipment; suspend high-risk work; cease crane operations above defined wind speeds |
| Protect Goods | Elevate vulnerable goods; protect critical records; shut non-essential power |
| Stormwater | Activate stormwater controls; deploy sandbags/booms; isolate drains (where valved) |
| Reefer Continuity | Transfer to emergency power per prioritization matrix; manual temp checks/logs during outages |
| Post-Event | Structural/electrical/environmental inspections; document findings and corrective actions |

### 8.7 Person Overboard

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Alert | Shout "Man Overboard"; maintain constant visual; point at casualty |
| Equipment | Throw lifebuoy with light/line; deploy additional flotation and throw-bags |
| Vessels | Halt nearby craft unless directed; notify NPA Marine/Police; launch designated rescue craft if safe; approach leeward; avoid crush points |
| Recovery | Treat for hypothermia/aspiration; call ambulance; preserve evidence; debrief |

### 8.8 Confined Space Incident/Rescue

| Requirement | Procedure |
|-------------|-----------|
| Entry Protocol | Do not enter without permit and rescue plan; perform atmospheric testing; continuous monitoring |
| Rescue | Rescue only by trained team with retrieval systems and standby; use SCBA or supplied air as required; keep entry/exit logs |

### 8.9 Crane/Load Handling Incidents

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Isolate | Establish collapse zone; isolate area; confirm structural stability |
| Recovery | Do not move load until assessed; engage specialist recovery; investigate rigging failure; preserve lifting gear as evidence |

### 8.10 Cyber Incident Impacting Safety/Operations

| Response | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Incident Response | Activate incident response; isolate affected networks; move to manual controls for safety-critical systems (reefer monitoring, access control) per BCP; maintain paper logs; notify leadership and NPA if port operations affected |

### 8.11 Utility Loss/Black Start

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| Checklist | Black start checklist: verify generator readiness; initiate ATS/manual transfer; confirm priority loads (reefer racks, control room, lighting, comms); load shed per matrix; verify fuel autonomy and quality (water-in-fuel test); document times and loads |

#### Actions

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|

| Procedure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|

| Physical | No visible smoke/heat; structural integrity confirmed; utilities safe; spills neutralized/contained; drains protected |

#### Phased Re-entry

| Phase | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Essential | Essential personnel first |
| Full | Full re-energization via permit-to-work and hot work permits as applicable |

#### Post Re-entry

| Action | Description |
|--------|-------------|
