# GK & A Logistics Services Limited

## INTEGRATED OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH, SAFETY & WOMEN-LED "SAFETY-FIRST" POLICY

**Policy No.:** OHS-POL-001 │ **Effective Date:** November 1, 2025 │ **Review Date:** October 31, 2026

---

## 1. PURPOSE

To provide a unified framework for managing occupational health and safety (OHS) and embedding our Women-Led "Safety-First" Initiative at all GK & A operations—beginning with the NPA Lighter Terminal, Ikorodu—to achieve ZERO HARM to people, assets and the environment.

## 2. SCOPE

Applies to all GK & A permanent, contract and temporary workers, visitors, vendors and third parties on company-controlled sites, with initial rollout at Ikorodu Lighter Terminal.

## 3. POLICY STATEMENT & COMMITMENTS

GK & A is committed to:

• ZERO work-related injuries and illnesses.

• Full compliance with Nigeria's Health, Safety & Welfare at Work Act, the Factories Act, ISO 45001:2018 and other legal requirements.

• Proactive hazard identification, risk assessment and application of the hierarchy of controls.

• Safe design, procurement and maintenance of plant, equipment and work environments.

• Competent, empowered teams through targeted training, instruction and supervision.

• Employee consultation and active participation in OHS decision-making.

• Robust emergency preparedness and response capabilities.

• Regular monitoring, measurement, auditing and management review of OHS performance.

• Continual improvement, driven by data, feedback and industry best practices.

• Elevating women into safety leadership roles to champion our Safety-First culture.

## 4. WOMEN-LED "SAFETY-FIRST" INITIATIVE

### 4.1 Vision

Embed women-led safety leadership at every level to accelerate our ZERO HARM ambition and foster an inclusive, proactive safety culture.

### 4.2 Objectives

• Halve our Lost Time Injury Rate (LTIR) within 12 months.

• Double near-miss reporting to prevent incidents.

• Increase female representation in HSE leadership from 10% to 40% within 18 months.

• Engage all personnel in a collaborative safety network.

### 4.3 Governance & Roles

#### 4.3.1 Women's Safety Steering Committee

– Co-chaired by the MD/CEO and the Senior HSE Manager (female).

– Meets monthly to review performance, allocate resources and approve corrective actions.

#### 4.3.2 Female HSE Champions

– Each shift is led by a certified female Champion with authority to stop unsafe work.

– Champions conduct daily site-walks, hazard hunts and lead "Safe Hands" briefings.

## 5. ROLES & RESPONSIBILITIES

**Board & MD/CEO** – Champion OHS strategy, allocate resources and review performance quarterly.

**HSE Manager** – Maintain the integrated OHS Management System; lead risk assessments, audits, investigations and management reviews.

**Supervisors & Line Managers** – Enforce safe work procedures, conduct briefings, verify PPE use and support corrective actions.

**Employees, Contractors & Visitors** – Comply with OHS rules, stop unsafe work, wear PPE and report hazards immediately.

## 6. HAZARD IDENTIFICATION & RISK CONTROL

• Perform Job Safety Analyses (JSAs) and Permit-to-Work for high-risk tasks.

• Deploy women-led Safety Patrols for daily inspections via mobile checklists with real-time tagging and follow-up.

• Apply the hierarchy of controls to eliminate or mitigate risks.

## 7. TRAINING & COMPETENCY

• **Induction:** OHS & "Women in Maritime Safety" module for all new hires and contractors.

• **Refresher:** quarterly courses and specialized modules for high-risk roles.

• **Safe Hands Workshops:** monthly, female-facilitated sessions with continuous improvement via our digital Safety Portal.

• **Certification support** for female staff (e.g. NEBOSH IGC, OSHA 30).

• Maintain competency records; no unsupervised work until fully qualified.

## 8. INCIDENT REPORTING & INVESTIGATION

• Report all incidents and near-misses within 24 hours via the digital Incident Portal.

• Complete root-cause analyses within 72 hours and track corrective/preventive actions.

• Share lessons learned in weekly bulletins and monthly reviews.

## 9. EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS & RESPONSE

• Maintain and bi-annually test the Emergency Response Plan (ERP).

• Operate a 24/7, fully female-staffed Emergency Response Team trained in fire, spill control and first aid.

• Conduct quarterly multi-agency drills with NPA, Coast Guard and health services.

• Ensure first-aid stations, fire-fighting equipment and exits are fully functional.

## 10. MONITORING, AUDIT & MANAGEMENT REVIEW

**Key Performance Indicators (12-Month Targets):**

• Lost Time Injury Rate ≤ 0.5 per 200,000 hrs worked

• Near-Miss Reports ≥ 2× current baseline

• Female HSE Leaders ≥ 40% of supervisory roles

• Audit Close-Out Rate ≥ 95% within 30 days

• Monthly dashboards reviewed by the Steering Committee.

• Quarterly internal audits and annual external reviews.

• Annual management review by Board/MD.

## 11. RECOGNITION & REWARDS

• "Safety Champion of the Month" (gift + certificate).

• Annual "Women in Safety" Excellence Gala.

• Team bonuses for zero non-conformances in audits.

## 12. IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP

| Phase | Timeline | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| Mobilize | Month 1 | Appoint Steering Committee & Champions; launch communications campaign |
| Pilot | Months 2–4 | Roll out Safe Hands Workshops & Patrols; launch near-miss reporting |
| Scale | Months 5–12 | Expand training and KPI tracking; conduct first external ERT drill |
| Embed | Months 13–18 | Formalize career pathways; host inaugural "Women in Safety" Gala |
