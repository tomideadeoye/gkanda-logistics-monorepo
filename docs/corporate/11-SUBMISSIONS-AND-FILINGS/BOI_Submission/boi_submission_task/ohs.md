## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This Integrated Occupational Health & Safety Policy establishes GK & A’s commitment to achieving ZERO HARM to people, assets and the environment through an OHS Management System aligned with ISO 45001:2018 and Nigerian statutory requirements. The Women‑Led “Safety‑First” Initiative is embedded to accelerate culture change by elevating women into HSE leadership and frontline safety roles. The policy will be piloted at the NPA Ikorodu Lighter Terminal and scaled company‑wide. Key targets include halving LTIR within 12 months, doubling near‑miss reporting and raising female HSE leadership to 40% within 18 months.

## 1. PURPOSE

To provide a unified framework for managing occupational health and safety (OHS) across GK & A operations and to embed the Women‑Led “Safety‑First” Initiative at all levels—commencing with the NPA Lighter Terminal, Ikorodu—aiming for ZERO HARM to employees, contractors, visitors, assets and the environment. The policy aligns with ISO 45001:2018, Nigeria’s Health, Safety & Welfare at Work Act and the Factories Act.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- All GK & A permanent, contract and temporary workers.
- All visitors, vendors and third parties on GK & A controlled sites.
- All sites and activities under GK & A control, including the pilot at Ikorodu Lighter Terminal.

**Exclusions:** Activities outside GK & A direct control except where contractual obligations require compliance with this policy.

---

## 3. POLICY STATEMENT & COMMITMENTS

GK & A is committed to:
- ZERO work‑related injuries and illnesses.
- Full compliance with Nigeria’s Health, Safety & Welfare at Work Act, the Factories Act, relevant NPA regulations and ISO 45001:2018.
- Proactive hazard identification, risk assessment and systematic application of the hierarchy of controls.
- Safe design, procurement and maintenance of plant, equipment and work environments.
- Competent, empowered teams through targeted training, instruction and supervision.
- Employee consultation and active participation in OHS decision‑making.
- Robust emergency preparedness and response capabilities.
- Regular monitoring, measurement, auditing and management review of OHS performance.
- Continual improvement driven by data, feedback and industry best practices.
- Elevating women into safety leadership roles to champion our Safety‑First culture.

All managers and supervisors shall take ownership for implementing this policy. Employees and contractors are required to comply.

### 4.1 Vision

Embed women‑led safety leadership at every level to accelerate our ZERO HARM ambition and foster an inclusive, proactive safety culture.

## 6. HAZARD IDENTIFICATION & RISK CONTROL

**Risk Management Framework**
- Identify hazards, analyse and evaluate risks, determine and implement controls, monitor and review effectiveness.
- Risk scoring: Likelihood (1–5) × Consequence (1–5) = Risk Rating. Priority given to high ratings.

**Job Safety Analysis (JSA)**
- Required for all high hazard tasks. Steps: task breakdown, hazards, risk rating, controls, responsible person, sign‑off. JSA template in Appendix D.

**Permit‑to‑Work (P2W)**
- Mandatory for high risk tasks: hot work, confined space, electrical, excavation, lifting, working at height.
- Permit types: Routine, High‑risk, Emergency, Special.
- Authorisation levels: Supervisor (routine), Site Manager (high risk), Senior HSE Manager (complex).

**Women‑led Safety Patrols**
- Daily patrols by female HSE Champions using mobile checklists and real‑time tagging.
- Patrol KPI: 100% daily coverage; 95% close‑out within 30 days.

**Hierarchy of Controls**
- Apply elimination, substitution, engineering controls, administrative controls and PPE in that order.

**Standard Safe Work Procedures (SWPs)**
- Core SWPs: working at height, confined space entry, LOTO, crane & lifting operations, manual handling, vehicle movement, hazardous substances, hot work.

**Contractor Management**
- Prequalification, method statements, induction, supervision and permit adherence; require proof of training and insurance.

**Human Factors & Ergonomics**
- Fatigue management, heat stress measures, job rotation and manual handling improvements.

**Environmental Controls**
- Spill prevention, bunding, waste segregation and regulatory compliance.

## 8. INCIDENT REPORTING & INVESTIGATION

**Reporting**
- All incidents, near‑misses and unsafe conditions are to be reported within 24 hours via the digital Incident Portal or verbally to the supervisor.
- Immediate notification for major incidents to HSE Manager and Steering Committee.

**Investigation**
- **Categorise:** LTI, MTI, RWC, near‑miss.
- **Investigation team:** HSE Manager, Site Manager, female HSE Champion, and SME as needed.

**Documentation & Learning**

## DETAILED PROCEDURES (Operational Guidance)

**Note:** Detailed SOPs and controlled documents shall be developed from these procedures and maintained by HSE.

**Permit‑to‑Work (P2W)**
- **Purpose:** control high risk work.
- **Scope:** hot work, confined space, electrical, excavation, lifting, working at height.
- **Process:** request → JSA → risk assessment → permit issuance with controls → execution → handback → close‑out.
- **Validity:** defined on permit; re‑issue required at shift change.
- **Emergency permit:** use emergency permit with post‑work review.

**Job Safety Analysis (JSA)**
- **Steps:** identify task → break into steps → identify hazards → rate risks → determine controls → assign responsibility → sign‑off.
- **Review:** annually, after changes or incidents.

**Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)**
- Isolate all energy sources; authorised persons apply locks/tags; verification before work; documented handover for group LOTO.

**Working at Height**
- Pre‑use inspection of MEWPs and scaffolds; use of harnesses and certified anchor points; trained rescue plan.

**Crane, Lifting & Slinging**
- Lifting plan for lifts >1 tonne or complex lifts; competent crane operator and signaller; exclusion zones enforced.

**Confined Space**
- Permit required, continuous atmospheric monitoring, standby attendant and rescue plan.

**Hazardous Substances**
- SDS maintained; safe storage, handling and spill response procedures.

**Traffic & Vehicle Management**
- Segregated routes, speed limits, traffic marshals and pre‑start vehicle checks.

**Manual Handling & Ergonomics**
- Use of mechanical aids, manual handling training and job redesign to reduce risk.

**Fatigue Management**
- Defined shift limits, rest breaks, fatigue reporting and risk assessment for high fatigue activities.

**PPE Matrix**
- PPE requirements by task, including female sizing and procurement processes (Appendix J).
