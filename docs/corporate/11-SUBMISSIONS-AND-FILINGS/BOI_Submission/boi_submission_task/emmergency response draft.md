INTRODUCTION
- Status and oversight: This Plan is a jointly endorsed, auditable operational control developed under Nigerian Ports Authority (NPA) oversight. Procedures, notification chains, and role interfaces have been reviewed with NPA and are mandatory for all personnel and contractors on GK & A-controlled areas of the NPA Lighter Terminal, Ikorodu.
- Enforcement: Roles and accountabilities are assigned in writing. Compliance is continuously monitored. Violations are escalated through disciplinary protocols and may trigger regulatory reporting to NPA and relevant authorities.
- Joint execution: GK & A and NPA Safety Officers shall co-lead emergency activations, joint drills, audits, debriefs, and corrective actions. NPA retains authority to intervene and direct operations where necessary to protect life, environment, and port security.

1. PURPOSE
This Plan establishes an integrated, zero-harm emergency management framework to:
- Protect people, property, cargo integrity, and the environment.
- Prevent escalation and minimize business interruption.
- Achieve and evidence compliance with: NPA directives; ISPS Code; IMDG Code; ISO 45001 and ISO 14001 principles; ILO/IMO Code of Practice for Safety and Health in Ports; Lagos State and Federal environmental obligations.
- Apply the most stringent requirement when standards overlap.
Measurable objectives
- Zero fatalities; zero serious injuries (life-altering).
- External notifications initiated within 5 minutes of emergency declaration.
- Full headcount reconciliation within 10 minutes of evacuation order.
- Drill participation ≥ 90% of on-duty personnel; corrective actions closed ≤ 30 days.
- No uncontrolled discharge to water; all spills contained within the site boundary.

2. SCOPE
Applies 24/7 to GK & A-controlled operations within the NPA Lighter Terminal, Ikorodu:
- Quay, jetties, cargo yards, and operational zones under NPA regulation.
- Warehouses and open storage areas.
- Reefer container stacks, plug-in racks, and associated power/refrigeration systems.
Interfaces and inclusions
- Covers all employees, contractors, subcontractors, visitors, clients, and third-party carriers.
- Includes tenants or third parties while in GK & A-controlled zones.
- NPA Safety Officers participate in activations, drills, debriefs, and reviews.
Exclusions
- Non-GK & A premises beyond defined interface points; however, GK & A shall support NPA-directed mutual aid and information sharing as requested.

3. DEFINITIONS
- Emergency: Unplanned event threatening life, environment, assets, cargo, or operations.
- Incident Commander (IC): On-scene authority responsible for declaring and managing the incident; approves re-entry.
- Deputy IC: Authorized alternate who assumes command if needed.
- Emergency Response Team (ERT): Trained responders for Fire, HAZMAT/Spill, Medical, Refrigerant/Technical, Security.
- Public Information Officer (PIO): Sole authorized spokesperson for external communications.
- Muster Point: Designated safe assembly location for headcount after evacuation.
- Exclusion Zone: Controlled perimeter restricting access to trained, authorized personnel only.
- ALARP: As Low As Reasonably Practicable; risk threshold for operations and rescue.
- Refrigerant: Substances used in reefers (e.g., A1: R-134a, R-404A, R-452A; A2L where applicable).
- Shelter-in-place: Controlled indoor protection procedure during external airborne or security threats.
- Command Post (CP): Primary/secondary locations where IC and key staff coordinate response.
- Staging Area: Controlled location where resources and teams assemble before tasking.

7. ACTIVATION, NOTIFICATION, AND INITIAL ACTIONS
7.1 Triggers
- Fire/explosion/smoke alarm; chemical or fuel spill; refrigerant alarm/odor; serious injury; security incident; severe weather/flooding/lightning; structural compromise; person overboard; extensive power loss affecting reefers.
7.2 First five minutes (mandatory)
- Raise alarm: pull station or radio in clear text “EMERGENCY – [Location] – [Type].”
- IC or senior-most present declares emergency; stands up Command Post; assigns scribe; opens Incident Log with time-stamps.
- Dispatch ERT; confirm PPE (SCBA for unknown atmospheres).
- Notify NPA Port Control immediately; escalate to Fire/EMS/Police/LASEMA/LASWA/NIWA as needed per matrix.
- Lock down or open gates as appropriate to incident type to secure site or facilitate emergency ingress.
7.3 First ten minutes
- Order evacuation or shelter-in-place as conditions dictate.
- Establish exclusion zones; deny entry.
- Achieve full headcount reconciliation at muster points within 10 minutes; identify missing persons; do not start rescue unless risk is ALARP and approved by IC.
- Issue SITREP 1 to leadership and NPA.
7.4 Alerting systems
- Site sirens/PA; UHF radios (Channels: 1 Ops, 2 ERT, 3 Command); SMS/app alerts. Backups: air horns, runners.

8. EMERGENCY RESPONSE PROCEDURES
General priorities: Protect life; isolate hazards; prevent escalation; protect environment; preserve evidence.

8.1 Fire
- PPE: Full structural gear and SCBA for interior/close approach.
- Electrical/reefer fires: Isolate power first if safe; use CO2/DCP; avoid water on live components.
- Flammable liquid fires: Apply foam; protect drains; deploy booms to capture runoff; avoid fluorinated foams unless no alternative; prefer fluorine-free foam (F3).
- Warehouse/stack fires: Establish 360° size-up; protect exposures; set collapse zones; use TIC to confirm knockdown; ventilate cautiously to prevent flashover; compartment doors stay closed until cooled and monitored.
- Post-fire: Fire watch 60 minutes minimum; water/runoff containment and sampling; document all actions and foam use.

8.2 Hazardous Spills/HAZMAT
- Approach upwind/upstream; identify via SDS/IMDG; don chemical-resistant PPE; consider SCBA for unknown/toxic atmospheres.
- Control: Stop the source; isolate ignition sources; ground/bond for flammables; deploy drain covers, booms, absorbents, temporary bunds; protect watercourses.
- Collection: Overpack drums, labeled containers; segregate waste; store in designated HAZMAT area for licensed disposal.
- Notifications: NPA Port Control; LASEPA/NESREA where thresholds met; record volumes and materials.
- Decontamination: Establish gross decon for responders/casualties when required before EMS handover.

8.3 Refrigerant Leaks (Reefer Stacks)
- Hazards: A1—oxygen displacement; cold burns. A2L—mild flammability; ignition risk; decomposition products if heated.
- Immediate controls:
  - Evacuate immediate area; mark no-ignition zone.
  - Minimum cordon: 50 m if concentration and wind unknown; adjust based on readings.
  - Prohibit hot work; isolate electrical power to affected unit(s).
- Monitoring:
  - Use calibrated refrigerant leak detector (NDIR/IR for halocarbons) plus multi-gas meter.
  - Alarm set-points: warn at 10% LFL for A2L; evacuate/cordon at ≥20–25% LFL; manage A1 as asphyxiant—use SDS OEL (often ~1,000 ppm TWA; confirm per SDS).
- Response:
  - Ventilate naturally or mechanically; ensure continuous ventilation in reefer alleys; interlock hot work shutdown where fixed detection exists.
  - Only qualified technicians to isolate/repair; recover refrigerant to approved cylinders; record loss and service details.
- Re-occupy only when:
  - O2 19.5–23.5%; LEL < 10%; refrigerant below OEL per SDS; two consecutive stable readings 5–10 minutes apart.

8.4 Medical Emergencies
- Scene safety; announce “Medical Emergency – General Hospital Ikorodu ; deploy AED/trauma kit.
- DRSABC; control bleeding; burns: cool with clean water (≥20 minutes); chemical exposures: flush ≥15 minutes and consult SDS.
- Heat stress: remove from heat, active cooling, hydration, monitor vitals.
- Coordinate LASAMBUS/112/767; guide ambulance through designated gate; ensure casualty/witness logs; decon equipment after use.

8.5 Security Incidents
- Lockdown/access control per PFSP; badge deactivation if required; ensure CCTV preservation and evidence handling.
- Bomb/suspicious package: follow checklist; evacuate upwind; avoid radios within 15 m of suspect device.
- Intrusion/theft: do not pursue; preserve scene; notify NPA Port Police.
- Civil disturbance/armed threat: shelter-in-place; safe room protocols; code words for silent alerts; comply with law enforcement.
- PIO manages all external comms; staff prohibited from social media posting.

8.6 Severe Weather and Flooding
- Monitoring: lightning alerts; wind thresholds; tidal/surge data.
- Actions:
  - Secure loose cargo/equipment; suspend high-risk work; cease crane operations above defined wind speeds.
  - Elevate vulnerable goods; protect critical records; shut non-essential power.
  - Activate stormwater controls; deploy sandbags/booms; isolate drains (where valved).
- Reefer continuity: Transfer to emergency power per prioritization matrix; manual temp checks/logs during outages.
- Post-event: Structural/electrical/environmental inspections; document findings and corrective actions.

8.7 Person Overboard
- Shout “Man Overboard”; maintain constant visual; point at casualty.
- Throw lifebuoy with light/line; deploy additional flotation and throw-bags.
- Halt nearby craft unless directed; notify NPA Marine/Police; launch designated rescue craft if safe; approach leeward; avoid crush points.
- Recovery: Treat for hypothermia/aspiration; call ambulance; preserve evidence; debrief.

8.8 Confined Space Incident/Rescue
- Do not enter without permit and rescue plan; perform atmospheric testing; continuous monitoring.
- Rescue only by trained team with retrieval systems and standby; use SCBA or supplied air as required; keep entry/exit logs.

8.9 Crane/Load Handling Incidents
- Establish collapse zone; isolate area; confirm structural stability.
- Do not move load until assessed; engage specialist recovery; investigate rigging failure; preserve lifting gear as evidence.

8.10 Cyber Incident Impacting Safety/Operations
- Activate incident response; isolate affected networks; move to manual controls for safety-critical systems (reefer monitoring, access control) per BCP; maintain paper logs; notify leadership and NPA if port operations affected.

8.11 Utility Loss/Black Start
- Black start checklist: verify generator readiness; initiate ATS/manual transfer; confirm priority loads (reefer racks, control room, lighting, comms); load shed per matrix; verify fuel autonomy and quality (water-in-fuel test); document times and loads.

9. EVACUATION, SHELTER-IN-PLACE, ACCOUNTABILITY, AND RE-ENTRY
9.1 Evacuation
- Triggers: fire; HAZMAT/refrigerant; structural threat; person overboard in proximity; security per IC; severe weather.
- Routes and muster points: Use primary/secondary routes to four muster points located upwind/up-current and away from reefer banks and HAZMAT stores; maps in Appendix B; muster capacity limits posted.
- Assisted evacuation: Buddy system; evacuation chairs where needed; Wardens/Marshals sweep zones, stairwells, amenities.
- Vehicles: Do not use unless directed for controlled egress or to clear critical paths.
9.2 Shelter-in-place
- Trigger: external toxic plume; civil disturbance/armed threat; lightning with no safe egress.
- Actions: move indoors; close doors/windows; switch off ventilation; seal gaps; stay tuned to Channel 3; await all-clear.
9.3 Accountability
- Headcount completed within 10 minutes using T-cards, access logs, visitor registers, PTW lists; report missing persons with last known location; IC approves search-and-rescue only when risk is ALARP.
9.4 Re-entry and re-energization
- IC grants re-entry with NPA concurrence after:
  - Atmosphere safe: O2 19.5–23.5%; LEL < 10%; CO < 25 ppm; H2S/other toxics below OEL per SDS; refrigerant below OEL per SDS; two consecutive stable readings 5–10 minutes apart.
  - No visible smoke/heat; structural integrity confirmed; utilities safe; spills neutralized/contained; drains protected.
- Phased re-entry: essential personnel first; full re-energization via permit-to-work and hot work permits as applicable.
- Post re-entry: 60-minute fire watch and status update to all staff/clients as needed.

11. RESOURCES, EQUIPMENT, AND MAINTENANCE
11.1 Minimum critical inventory
- Firefighting: DCP/CO2/foam extinguishers by hazard class; hydrants/hose reels; fire pumps; foam trailer (F3 preferred); thermal imaging cameras.
- HAZMAT: spill kits (oil/chemical/universal), drain covers, absorbent booms, overpack drums, neutralizers.
- Detection: portable multi-gas meters (O2, LEL, CO, H2S), refrigerant detectors compatible with installed gases; fixed detection where installed.
- Respiratory/Rescue: SCBA sets with spare cylinders; escape hoods; lifebuoys with lights/lines every 25–50 m; throw-bags; quay ladders; rescue boat readiness.
- Medical: first-aid kits, trauma kits, AEDs, stretchers/spine boards, eyewash/shower stations.
- Power: emergency generators with ATS prioritizing reefer circuits, control room, lighting, comms; fuel autonomy ≥ 48 hours; critical spares (ATS controller, breakers, reefer plugs/connectors).
11.2 Maintenance and assurance
- CMMS-driven inspections; monthly visual checks; quarterly functional tests; detector bump tests per shift use and calibration per manufacturer; SCBA flow tests; AED pads/battery within expiry; hydrant/flow tests; generator monthly load tests; quarterly full-load bank tests.
- Color-tag system: in service/out of service; only authorized technicians may tag equipment; immediate notification to IC/HSE when life-safety equipment is OOS; rectify within defined SLA.
- Third-party service contracts for pumps, detectors, SCBA, AEDs, generators, reefer systems. Certificates retained.

12.2 Core training (all staff)

12.3 Role-specific competencies
- ERT: advanced firefighting; HAZMAT spill control; SCBA; confined space awareness; water rescue elements; incident command (ICS 100/200); ICS 300 for IC/Deputy; refrigerated cargo hazards.
- Operators: forklift/crane; banksman/signaller; load securement; work at height; LOTO.
- Technicians: refrigerant safety incl. A2L awareness; electrical safety; arc-flash awareness; hot work permit; LOTO.
- Wardens/Marshals: evacuation leadership; headcount procedures; crowd management; assisted evacuation.
- PIO/Comms: crisis comms; media handling; message maps; rumor control.
12.4 Drill program and cadence
- Quarterly site-wide evacuations (include a night-shift and a no-notice drill annually).
- Semi-annual HAZMAT and refrigerant-leak drills (at least one A2L scenario).
- Annual multi-agency full-scale exercise with NPA and emergency services (include water rescue).
- Quarterly table-top exercises for leadership/supervisors with injects (power loss + refrigerant + medical).
12.5 KPIs and records
- Headcount within 10 minutes; response-to-containment times met; ≥ 90% on-duty participation; corrective actions closed ≤ 30 days; requalification before expiry. Training matrix and certificates maintained.
